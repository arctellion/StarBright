    "answer": "The Traveller Mapping System (TMS) provides a structured hierarchy for detailing world surfaces, ranging from global overviews to individual square kilometers [1, 2]. This system is governed by the **MOARN** principle (**Map Only As Really Necessary**), encouraging referees and players to generate detail only when it becomes relevant to the adventure [3, 4].\n\n### The Mapping Hierarchy and Dimensions\nThe TMS organizes a world\u2019s surface into nested scales of hexagons. Each level of the hierarchy uses constant-sized hexes regardless of the planet's actual size, though larger worlds will contain a greater total number of these hexes [5, 6].\n\n| Map Level | Hex Diameter | Area/Description | Composition |\n| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |\n| **World Map** | N/A | A flattened icosahedron (20 triangles) [7]. | 20 World Triangles [5]. |\n| **World Triangle** | Variable | Approximately continental or oceanic in scale [5]. | Edge length in hexes equals World Size [8]. |\n| **World Hex** | **1,000 km** | Regional scale; identifies overall terrain types [5, 9]. | Contains **75 Terrain Hexes** [9]. |\n| **Terrain Hex** | **100 km** | Distant scale; used for initial exploration [5, 10]. | Contains **75 Local Hexes** [10]. |\n| **Local Hex** | **10 km** | Local scale; used for fine mapping of sites [11, 12]. | Contains **75 Single Hexes** [12]. |\n| **Single Hex** | **1 km** | The ultimate mapping scale [11, 13]. | Divided into 100-meter reference hexes [13]. |\n\n### The 75-Hex Structure\nIn this hierarchy, each larger hex is procedurally divided into **75 constituent hexes** of the next smaller scale [9, 10, 12]. Mathematically, this consists of **61 complete hexes**, plus **24 half-hexes** and **6 third-hexes** that comprise the edges and corners [9].\n\nFor tracking and random generation, these internal hexes are divided into two sets:\n*   **Black Numbered Hexes (BNH):** Numbered 11 through 66 [14, 15].\n*   **White Numbered Hexes (WNH):** Numbered 11 through 66 [14, 15].\n*   **Unnumbered Hexes:** Three hexes at the edges that default to the \"Overall Terrain\" of the parent hex [14, 15].\n\n### Procedural Terrain Generation\nMapping begins with the world's **Universal World Profile (UWP)** and its **Trade Classifications (TCs)**, which dictate the presence of features like ice caps, oceans, and cities [16, 17].\n\n#### 1. Generating the World Map\nThe referee selects a blank template based on World Size (1\u201320) and populates the 20 triangles following specific steps [17, 18]:\n*   **Hydrographics:** Calculate the number of Ocean Triangles by multiplying the Hydrographics digit by 2 (e.g., Hyd 4 means 8 ocean triangles) [17].\n*   **Land Features:** Place Mountains (1D per triangle), Chasms (Size x 1D per triangle), and Precipices (Size x 1) [17].\n*   **Climate Effects:** If the world is in a cold Habitable Zone (HZ), mark rows near the poles as Ice Caps [19].\n*   **Civilization:** Place Cities equal to the Population digit, and the main Starport in a logical location [20].\n\n#### 2.
